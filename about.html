About Us

For 20 years, Dynamic Tutors has been aiding students from lower-income and disadvantaged backgrounds, offering free programmes and tutoring to boost student performance and improve examination outcomes. Our work is supported by a team of dedicated volunteers, which includes university students from a wide range of backgrounds.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the academic requirements to volunteer as a tutor?

To tutor in our A-level programmes, you will require at least an B in the chosen subject at A-level or to be studying the subject at University.
For potential GCSE level tutors, we require you to meet either of the above requirements, or to have achieved at least a grade 8 in the subject at GCSE.

What is the time commitment for volunteers?

Our tutors typically spend one hour a week in preparation, and about two hours weekly in sessions during our programmes. Depending on your programme and subject, this might differ slightly.

What subjects are included in the programmes?

Our programmes typically include English, Maths, Physics, Chemistry and Biology at GCSE. The same are offered at A-level, along with History, Literature and Further Maths.

Will I be provided with training?

Yes! Our new tutors all undergo 3 half-days of training before joining their programmes. We also organise periodic refresher training sessions for existing tutors.

Where do the sessions take place?

For most programmes, sessions take place on the school premises of the participating school.
We also hold some programmes entirely online, which you can volunteer on if you don't live in one of the cities we operate in.

How can I report an observation or concern?

For safety or safeguarding-related issues, please email safeguarding@dynamic-tutors.co.uk.
For all other questions and comments, please email enquiries@dynamic-tutors.co.uk.
